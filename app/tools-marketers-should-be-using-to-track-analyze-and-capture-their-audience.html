Tools marketers should be using to track, analyze and capture their audience

Marketers are easily overwhelmed when it comes to choosing the right tools to get things done. Almost everyday there is a new marketing tool that’s being up voted on Product Hunt. As a marketer for a startup, or even a large corporate, there’s a lot on your plate: analytics, email, automation, A/B Testing, workflows, funnel optimization, metrics and now even growth hacking.

To actually get things done, you need to educate yourself on the tools that are available today. It’s important that you try and configure your own stack as a marketer. One that your startup or big company can benefit from.

In our previous post: 10 presentations every marketer should see, we briefly talked about shaping yourself like a T. We’re convinced that marketers can’t be jack of all trades. That means that every marketer needs to carve out his own learning curve. Make sure to read the following blogpost by Brian Balfour if you want to shape yourself like a T.

The same goes for choosing the right tools for your company or product. We tried to compile a list of tools that we use on a daily basis. We also recommend these to our startups.

With these tools you will be able to:

  • Track everything (visitors, events)
  • Analyze your acquisition, activation, retention, revenue, referral - funnel
  • Capture your leads
  • Do email marketing campaigns, based on the behavior of your visitors/signups
  • Do A/B testing
  • Automate some of your processes via workflows
  • Talk to your users
